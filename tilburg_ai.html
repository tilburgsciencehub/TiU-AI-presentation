2. How is AI currently used at Tilburg University?

How we use AI in teaching

  • Course design and management
    • Generating and/or improving syllabi and lecture outlines
    • Creating and revising exam questions
  • Content delivery
    • Finding examples and case studies
    • Improving slides, generating subtitles
  • Automation
    • Semi-automated grading
    • “Patchworking” different systems

We leverage AI to efficiently improve education through engaging, multimodal, and high-quality content.

How our students use AI

  • Editing and writing coach (e.g., finding good titles & writing abstracts, feedback on writing style, developing paragraph structures, synthesizing)
  • Ideation and problem solving (e.g., “which R package to use for X?”)
  • Fixing or annotating computer code (e.g., “I have a bug in this code - do you spot it?”)
  • A “virtual” teacher, 24/7, immediate response (e.g., “why is X important?”, “can you explain how X works?”)
  • Preparing for the labor market (e.g., redesigning resumes)
  • Realizing efficiencies (e.g., converting documents, taking over part of one’s job)

How do we use it in research?

  • Writing computer code (e.g., GitHub copilot)
  • Editing, proofreading (e.g., cutting words)
  • Automation (e.g., filling in forms, answering emails)
  • Ideation (e.g., generating outlines)
  • Research assistant (e.g., generating stimuli, summarizing articles)

3. What are new ways
in which we can use AI?

Automating the use of AI

  • API ecosystems (“linching pin” of the internet economy; e.g., Facebook, Spotify, Amazon Web Services)
  • ChatGPT is “just” an interface to OpenAI’s APIs (and its latest version, “GPT-4 API”, was just announced yesterday)
    • creativity, advanced reasoning (bar exam: 90th percentile, biology: 99th)
    • already used in education: student feedback (Khan Academy), conversations in foreign languages (Duolingo)

In rolling out AI in Tilburg, let’s think about (1) applying AI manually (“hey, here’s an exam question - can you create 5 different versions?”), and (2) automating the use of AI in scaling up successful use cases.

Future use cases for teaching

  • Low-hanging fruits
    • Aligning course content with “blended” learning strategy (e.g., updating syllabus, introducing more interactive moments)
    • “Recycling” existing course content (old lectures) by creative cutting, format conversion, transcripting, redeveloping
    • Improving assessment (e.g., more formative testing, generation of (open) questions)
  • Challenging but possible with today’s technology
    • Automating assessment (e.g., written feedback, automated grading)
    • Personal chat bot coach (e.g., study coach, career coach)
  • Still some investment needed (but we can do it!)
    • Personalization of admission requirements for courses and programs
    • “Algorithmic” and interdisciplinary study programs
    • Real-time scheduling (including students’ study rooms, “werkplekken”)
    • “Smart” budget and teaching allocation algorithms

Risks of AI for Tilburg University

Risks of NOT embracing AI

  • Losing students to other universities?
  • Falling behind in research productivity?
  • Not fulfilling our ambitious strategy (e.g., blended learning)
  • Lack of modernization of infrastructure and inability to automate
  • Possibility of students and staff using AI illegally
  • Not realizing efficiency gains (e.g., automation)

Risks when embracing AI

  • Copying and cheating (but: on-campus exams, “citability” of ChatGPT)
  • “Students do not learn X” (but: more feedback, more complicated problems)
  • Quality control when scaling up (but: firms can do it, already!)
  • Teaching delivery (but: also Powerpoint and Covid changed teaching)
  • Staffing (but: we can redefine roles)
  • Lack of trustworthiness (but: important 21st century skill to detect it!)

Conclusion

Conclusion

  • Our faculty already explores the use of AI in various aspects of education and research – how does Tilburg Unviersity facilitate them?
  • AI offers numerous benefits, such as personalization, automation, and time and cost savings
  • However, there are also potential risks and drawbacks that need to be addressed, such as ethical concerns and the need for quality control
  • To successfully integrate AI, Tilburg University needs to encourage use and experimentation, involve students and young faculty, educate its staff, and get its infrastructure ready

Embracing AI is crucial for the university to stay relevant and competitive in the rapidly evolving landscape of higher education and research
